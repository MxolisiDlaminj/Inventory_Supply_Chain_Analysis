
🧾 Dataset Structure (Column Definitions)
1. Date
•The transaction or record date.
•Useful for time-series analysis and trend identification.

2. Product_ID
•Unique identifier for each product.
•Used to group and track products across records.

3. Product_Name
•Name of the product (e.g., T-Shirt, Sneakers).
•Helps with readability and reporting.

4. Category
•Product category (e.g., Apparel, Footwear, Accessories).
•Useful for category-level analysis.

5. Warehouse
•Location where inventory is stored (e.g., Durban_WH, CapeTown_WH).
•Enables location-based analysis.

6. Stock_Level
•Current number of units available in inventory.
•Key metric for stock monitoring.

7. Reorder_Level
•Minimum threshold before stock needs replenishment.
•Used to trigger reorder decisions.

8. Reorder_Quantity
•Number of units to order when stock reaches reorder level.
•Helps in supply planning.

9. Supplier
•Supplier providing the product.
•Used for supplier performance analysis.

10. Lead_Time_Days
•Number of days taken by the supplier to deliver goods.
•Important for supply chain efficiency.

11. Unit_Cost
•Cost price per unit.
•Used for cost and profit calculations.

12. Unit_Price
•Selling price per unit.
•Used to calculate revenue.

13. Units_Sold
•Number of units sold on a given day.
•Indicates demand and sales performance.

14. Units_Ordered
•Number of units ordered from suppliers.
•Helps track replenishment activity.

15. Delivery_Status
•Status of the order (Delivered, Pending, Delayed).
